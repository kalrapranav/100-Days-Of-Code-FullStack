1) To strengthen their Reconnect program, Dell and Goodwill should take steps to ensure that electronic equipment is being recycled in the United States. This could include adding appropriate managers to track all of their e-waste. Another way to strengthen their Reconnect program is by partnering with other electronic companies, like Apple or Microsoft, to set up even more e-waste bins to further public awareness of recycling their electronic products,

2) The leaders of the United States may feel that this isn’t much of a problem since it is mainly hurting developing nations and doesn’t benefit them. Since they are the largest generator of e-waste, the U.S. doesn’t want to dispose of e-waste on U.S. soil when it can continue to export it out to smaller countries.

3) On October 23, 2018, the U.S. Department of Commerce released a proposal that would ban exports of unprocessed e-scraps and require stringent tracking procedures for exports that are still allowed. This would prohibit untested, end-of-life electronic devices from being exported out of the U.S. Unfortunately, it this was not enacted and companies are still free to legally export their e-waste. Despite this, states have enacted their own laws to safely dispose of hazardous e-waste. (source:https://resource-recycling.com/e-scrap/2018/11/01/federal-rule-would-restrict-u-s-e-scrap-exports/)

4) (Created Question 1) The United States can implement laws that can prevent the exportation of e-waste to developing nations. They could fine companies found guilty of exporting their e-waste. This can help create an effort for companies to find ways to safely dispose of e-waste on U.S. land.

5) (Created Question 2) Companies can implement e-waste CSR policies by examining whether it is purposeful for them to implement it. Electronic companies would greatly benefit from these policies, while paper companies may not. Determining whether the company needs these policies is a great way to start implementing this e-waste CSR policy. Within this policy, companies can prevent employees and higher ups in their company from throwing away electronic devices. They could place e-waste recycling bins to spread their CSR policy throughout the company. Companies will need to makes sure to remind employees of this policy to make sure it is being implemented effectively.
