Omar

Our case study gave me a lot of insight into how the international community has been handling e-waste and its exportation to developing nations. In these developing nations, when disposing of e-waste it is not done in an “environmentally sound manner”, which the emissions from this can cause health problems and affect the environment. While there has been some good steps taken to ensure that e-waste is safely disposed of, like the Basal Convention, there is still more to be done when it comes to dealing with this issue.

For one, the United States needs to wake up and ratify the Basal Convention, like other countries have done, to ensure that e-waste is being safely disposed within their country rather than being exported to developing countries. States in the U.S. have taken their own steps to ensure e-waste is safely disposed of which is a good step in the right direction. Companies like Dell and Goodwill have also taken the initiative of banning e-waste exportation and have been collecting e-waste from customers by having e-waste recycling bins in Goodwill stores. This is a good example of a company understanding what e-waste does to the world and what steps we should be taking to prevent e-waste problems. There is still much to be done with the world of e-waste, but when companies, states, or countries take the appropriate measures to safely dispose of e-waste, it makes the world a better place for everyone.

After going through this article, we have an understanding about the electronic waste (e-waste) and how companies from different countries are handling it. E-Waste is basically a term for electronics who are old and discarded electronic devices. Over 46 million tons of e-waste is produced all over the world and 50-80 percent of that is shipped to developing countries like China, India, Pakistan, the Philippines, and the Vietnam.

For recycling e-waste, it is important to do it safely but in such developing countries proper measures are not taken which is harmful to the environment and human beings. It is high time that United States ratify the Basal Convention so that safe decomposing of e-waste can be carried out within the country instead of sending it to other countries and letting them suffer. Couple companies like Dell and Goodwill, which are US based companies have banned exporting their e-waste. These 2 companies have set a good example to all the companies in the world to follow this path as they have been recycling their old products by collecting it from customers who do not use them.

I believe that companies who do still export their e-waste out of the country should be heavily fined and strict laws should be put in place by the government to make sure that those who breaks the law are punished.
